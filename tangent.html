📐 What is a Tangent?

Think of a tangent line as a **"high-five"** to a curve. It perfectly grazes the circle at exactly one single point without crossing through it.

  • Intersection: Exactly 1 point.
  • Real-world example: A skateboard wheel rolling smoothly on the flat ground. The ground is tangent to the wheel!

❌ What is a Non-Tangent?

If a line does anything else, it is non-tangent. Most commonly, it becomes a Secant line—cutting right through the circle like a sword.

  • Intersection: 2 points (cuts through) or 0 points (misses completely).
  • Real-world example: An arrow piercing right through an apple.
